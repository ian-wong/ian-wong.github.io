QAAFI

Staff Seating and Movement Database

Overview

Problem

The Queensland Alliance for Agriculture and Food Innovation (QAAFI) consists of 450 researchers, post graduate students and support staff across 15 buildings. Researchers and students have complex schedules, spending a few days a week at different facilities with in consistent. Currently, all staff movements are managed by an MS Access template database and reports are done manually. This system is limited by its inability to track future seating requirements and lack of visualisation of workspace.

Background

The Staff Seating and Movement Database is a web application that allows for an administrator (the client) to add employees and assign them to workstations for the visualisation of management. The administrator can generate PDF reports of the current seating environment. There will be a hierarchy to the reports, being able to be generated for the organisation, facility, building, floor. Also, the administrator can view workstation and hot desk usage statistics for current date and future dates.

Audience

This system is targeted to our client as he will be the only person who will have access and use this system to manage staff and seating. QAAFI staff members will only be able to view the floor plans availability through an overlay of workstation information

Role

UI Designer, UX Designer

Feature One: Dashboard

This is the home page of the system where it displays the seating availability of the whole QAAFI organisation. The two graphs goes into more in-depth for each building at both UQ and the Department of Agriculture and shows the unavailability and availability by using blue and green. It also shows the specific number of seats taken in the current day and can also look back to the history of available seats.

Feature Two: Detailed Statistics

A in-depth availability of each building is presented when the user clicks on any of the building from the graph in the home page. It shows the number of staff, the amount of free seats, and number of hot desks available. Hot desks are desks that many staff can use and are not dedicated to a specific staff.

Feature Three: Creating New Users

The administrator can create a new staff member/user to be able to be allocated to a seat.

Feature Four: Floor plan

The whole floor plan of each building can be viewed with both available and allocated seats shown. The seats are represented by a circle dot where the occupied is navy and available seats are green

Design Process

The design methodology Scrum was used throughout this project because it is an iterative, incremental process which helped the team consistently develop work to be able to be aligned with the client. It provided a good structure, rules and responsibility as the team was in charge creating and adapting the work. Each sprint was two weeks long where features were developed frequently and in line with the clients expectations.

Task Management: Trello

A Trello board was created to track and manage all the work so all members of the team and client can see and contribute to the work board. The product backlog was first created and prioritised with the client to gather all the requirements correctly before starting the development. Using the product backlog, specific tasks were generated for the whole team to work on during the sprint.

Communication

Fortnightly sprint meetings were held with all the team and the client at the University of Queensland to communicate and review the work that was completed during the sprint. This helped both the team and the client have an understanding of what is being developed, the progress of the project, any problems that may have occurred and any necessary changes that needed to made.

Wireframes

I created wireframes during the first sprint for the initial design of the main page. The first wireframe consisted of the details of all the staff members for that specific facility and floor is displayed specifically the staff's name, facility, floor and seat is shown on the sidebar. This did not prove to be good as a lot of information will be displayed when there are many staff allocated and made it difficult to see the amount of seats available.

The second wireframe consisted of having the data of the seats available and unavailable for the specific floor and then list the staff that are currently allocated when the user clicked on the used category. The third wireframe showed the listed staff members with bare minimum details such as there name or staff ID and when clicked upon more details of the staff will display.

Low-fidelity Prototype

After completing the wireframes, the client reviewed and suggested that having a dashboard interface with all the statistics of the seating would be more beneficial for the home page so that all the information is available to view in the beginning.

To iterate the design of the homepage, I created a mockup design of having graphs from both UQ and the department showing the availability of each building. Above the graphs were the floor plan for the building, current amount of staff and the date.

High-fidelity Prototype

Through iteration, this was the final design for the high fidelity prototype. You can see aspects from the low fidelity prototype that was implemented and what was changed to be the end result.

Final Outcome

Overall, the client was very impressed and happy with the work that Team Tempus had produced for the semester. Even though this was a high-fidelity prototype, the ability to conduct and use the features that was requested at the beginning of the scope created a sense of what the client could eventually use when fully developed. It was a fantastic opportunity to work in a project with a real client and experience professional work in an university environment.
