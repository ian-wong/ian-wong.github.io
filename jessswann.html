Jess Swann

Personal trainer website

Overview

Problem

Jess needed a personal website to inform people about her business and what purpose she brings to every client she trains. It is a company information site where she can share with people so that they understand more about her and what she does. She also wanted to be able to share her experience with blog posts and sell programs and any merch she develops in the future.

Background

This website tells people about Jess's business Habit Health and Fitness and what she provides as a personal trainer. There are free recipes that she writes to share for anyone who wants to be able to have a balanced diet. Furthermore, upcoming merch and programs will be available to be purchased and delivered to anyone interested.

Audience

It is targeted towards anyone who goes to the gym and trains hard but may want extra motivation or knowledge about their training, nutrition and coaching. This is aimed for people already are or want to be more reliant and confident in the gym.

Role

Web Design, Research, Product Design, UI Designer, UX Designer

Website

www.jessswann.com.au

Home page

Information about her programs and recipes are provided to let the users know some details about her business and what is available. Her Instagram feed is shown for users to follow her journey and get a brief look at her posts. Testimonials can be easily read to supply users some insight from the client's perspective.

Shop

Apparel, merch, programs and online coaching will be available to be purchased from the shop. Eventually, more products will be announced to come to the store.

Recipes

Find a number of recipes that Jess has created and love to eat to help her hit her daily macro-nutrition target.

Design Process

As this was a new project that a client has proposed I followed the process of Gathering Requirements, Research, Sketches, Wireframe, Mockup Design, Prototype and Testing. This step by step process helped me understand Jess's business and what she wanted to accomplish with this website. It was also an indicator of where I was with this project in terms of the stages to complete this project.

Gather Requirements

I conducted a stakeholder interview with Jess to get an idea of everything about her business so that I can figure out how I can build the website with respect and care for her business. To help me understand, I asked questions around the topics:

  • Why do you need this website?
  • Who is this targeted towards?
  • What are your values and the values of your business?
  • How are success measured for the project?
From these questions, I recognised her problem with not having that online presence besides Instagram for users to be easily able find information about her. Additionally, Jess pitched her ideas and discussed what needs to be completed. The main focuses of this website are to have a place where she could write recipes, sell her programs, sell her merchandise, eventually be able to post blogs and set up online 1-on-1 training sessions for clients.

Research

I started to find serveral different personal trainer websites by searching "Best Personal Trainer Websites" on google. This was to get a feel of what the style, layout, colour of the interface was like with PT websites which provided me with many ideas and compositions of designs for Jess's website. I did a competitors analysis of each website to compare the similarities and differences of the information provided and how the information was portrayed. Some of my inspirations for this project were:

  • Hannah Dales: https://www.hannahdales.com/
  • Slaywithloulou: https://www.slaywithloulou.com/
  • Jill Brown Fitness: http://www.jillbrownfitness.com/

Wireframe and Sketches

With the requirements and the research complete, I created quick sketches to drop down my initial ideas that will be used as a base of design for the wireframes and improve upon. I completed the wireframes on AdobeXD focusing on the layout and the position of the content. The page that I was most concerned or needed more time to design was the home page since it was the first page that the users will be presented with and the most important information had to be displayed.

Mockup Designs

I decided to use the same light pink (#F7BDDB) from the Habit logo for the colour scheme to keep consistent with the design and colour. It highlights the important area of content leading the users to look at the information presented. After designing the first iteration of the mockup, I presented to Jess some of the different designs that I had created and wanted her to select which one was better compared to the rest.

Test

After the completion of the prototype, I conducted some user testing sessions. My goals were to:

  • See the steps the user takes navigating between each page.
  • Observe any pain points that arose for the user.
  • Is Jess satisfied with the current design and requirements?

In summary, based on the feedback given there were improvements that needed to be done in terms of having more emphasis on Jess's Instagram to be shown on the page. Jess wanted her feed to be on the home page as she wanted more views and followers on her Instagram. Another change that Jess wanted to make was to have a generic shop that not only sells online programs but also products she has in mind for the future such as apparel and ebooks. She decided to forego with publishing any blogs for now since she is busy with other projects so I hid the blog page from the public, waiting for the time it needs to be reopened. There was no pain points that the users experienced during the session and enjoyed how easy it was to find the information on the page.

Final Outcome

Overall, this was a really fun and enjoyable first project that I completed as a freelancer after completing university. This was a good experience for me to learn about the whole process of getting real clients and solving their problems by applying what I learnt over the past years to a real-life situation. Some improvements that I will make in future will be handling the communication with the client better, I did not give weekly updates on the progress of the website and was not confident in asking some questions at the beginning, making me seem like I did not know what I was doing. I know that this is my first project with a client so I know that I can improve and fix these mistakes in the future. Some struggles that I had to face when I was developing in Wix was due to responsiveness issues where the layout and position of the content was different for every screen size so, to fix this I could not design outside the dotted line border within Wix.

It was amazing to see that Jess loves her website and appreciative of the work that I put in. She is an amazing client to work with and an even better trainer.
